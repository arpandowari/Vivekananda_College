ABOUT THE LIBRARY

Vivekananda College Central Library started its journey since the inception of college in 1950. The Central Library is situated on the second floor of the Arts and Commerce building. Library being the heart of any academic institution, it aims to provide all the essential services to its patrons. The college library reflects a fascinating combination of tradition and modernity. It houses about 45,000 books on various subjects -literature, history, philosophy, science, social science, commerce and management and so on. Another glorious part of our library is that it boasts of a number of very old manuscripts in Sanskrit literature as well as rare books in English and Bengali literature, history and philosophy. There is a special collection and a good number of reference books in the library collection. Very recently, the library system became automated by Koha LMS and facilitates the patron with web-OPAC services. With the advent of ICT, libraries are no longer confined within the four walls. E-resources nowadays are equally essential to facilitate the patrons going beyond the physical space of the library and to provide seamless service. So, subscription to NLIST service (e-resources through INFLIBNET) and eBooks subscription to Oxford University Press have been initiated by our college library. There is another point to be highlighted that there is a spacious Reading Room in Central Library having the capacity to accommodate 150 students to read at a time providing full open access system.

AREA AND ARRANGEMENT:

Central Library is housed in the second floor of Arts and Commerce Block comprising around 6000 sq. ft area and consists of different sections for smooth running of library services.

RULES AND REGULATIONS:

  • A student can take two (2) books at a time for fourteen (14) days against a Book Issue Card.
  • A student can reissue the book for another seven (7) days if he / she submit the book within the scheduled date and if there is no such demand for the said book.
  • A fine of Rs.1 will be charged per book per day.
  • Books once issued shall not be taken back on the same day.
  • Books lost, damaged, defaced or injured in any other way shall be replaced by the borrower.
  • A student of any discipline can issue one (1) book against Reading Room Card as On site check-out but he / she has to submit the book within 4 P.M. of the same day failing which a fine of Rs.5/- per book per day will be charged.
  • Students have to keep their belongings at the Bag Counter before entering the Reading Room.
  • Before leaving the library each and every student must satisfy himself as to whether the book (s) lent to him are in good condition and if not, he /she shall immediately bring the matter to the notice of the Librarian.
